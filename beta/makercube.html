Brief

As part of a course project, my team and I met with Makercube, a makerspace in Langley, BC to help determine design opportunities for improvement in either internal or client-facing operations.

After 2 months of research using methods including cultural probes and a co-design workshop, my team devised a digital solution (fig. 1) that could help makercube in their pursuit of creating a community with greater knowledge sharing and training, as well as incentivizing members to gain skills at their own pace.

Research

One of our methods of research were cultural probes. A cultural probe is a qualitative method of research that identifies the ways in which the user experience is affected, both positively and negatively.

Our cultural probe (fig. 2) involved three activities: a sketchbook with prompts, photo frames to help participants document process on their active projects, and a map which they could annotate and plan the ideal makerspace.

After collecting the completed cultural probes and synthesizing the results, we found that community engagement and knowledge sharing (fig. 3) were the main points of focus for the members and facilitators at Makercube. We decided to design a solution to help cultivate, maintain, and maximize these connections.

The Solution

Our solution consisted of two parts: the companion app and the digial kiosk. The feature common to both is a feature that lets members mark themselves available and accessible to help other members with a discipline in which they are an expert. This was designed to drive up engagement and knowledge sharing in the Makercube community.

The Makercube companion app (fig. 4) lets users chat with other makers in the space who have marked themselves as available for help. Other features include viewing and registering for upcoming workshops, and a hyperlinked, wiki-style handbook.

The kiosk interfact allows users to sign in using their RFID-enabled membership card or key fob, depending on the type of membership. Once they sign in, the user can set their availability (fig. 5), which would correspond to their availability as it appears on the Companion app.

Summary

At the end of this semester-long process, my team and I were able to not only effectively diagnose and communicate a business problem, but also deliver a solution that delivers an applied system that aligns with Makercube's priority: cultivating a community of creatives where exchange of ideas and skills is encouraged. The founders at Makercube have expressed interest in implementing our design and taking this project out of its originally intended course context.
